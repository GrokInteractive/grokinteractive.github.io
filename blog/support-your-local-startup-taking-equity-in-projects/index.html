Support Your Local Startup - Taking Equity in Projects

by Jason Straughan    Sep 14, 2015    Software development Developers

As developers, we are constantly pitched on trading our work for equity in the next Facebook killer. Over the past few years, I have been approached by countless people proffering NDAs in exchange for the privilege to develop their idea for free, with promises of billionaire status in the near future.

Full Disclosure: we never take these "deals".

We do, however, take some equity in many of our clients that are early stage startups. No one wants to work for free in hopes of a better tomorrow, but taking a reduced rate in exchange for a small amount of equity can be beneficial to both parties.

The startup ecosystem is constantly in need of developers to help their teams roll-out their minimum viable product (MVP). Even after raising some seed money, their budgets are usually tight and require some creativity to make work with a development shop. We have found that offering a lower rate with some equity in exchange can be the recipe for a great relationship.

In this model, the client receives high quality software at a rate they can afford, and we still are able to have cash flow while being a part of several startups. Our team members love working with founders on a new product, and being able to spin up a solid dev team for a couple of months is something many startups cannot afford to do, especially if they do not need a full dev team post-launch.

Early stage startups are a vital part of the software sector. They are inspiring, and working with them is always a great time. Developers love solving unique problems, just like the entrepreneurs that found tech companies. We find these clients to be some of the best to work with, and they invigorate our teams, making everyone better at their craft.

Supporting your local (or distant) startup is simple: if you like the team and the product, consider a reduced rate in exchange for equity when proposing your service. We think it is worth it.
